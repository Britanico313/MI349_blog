Cancer Rates In Auto Workers

Published on November 5, 2023 by Brian A Toscano

In a recent study, researchers delved into the potential health risks associated with exposure to motor oils and fumes among workers in the United Auto Workers - General Motors (UAW-GM) cohort as well as Valvolines and Jiffy Lubes across the country spanning from 1973 to 2015. The study, which included 100,000 workers, aimed to assess the incidence of multiple different types of cancer, with a focus on digestive, respiratory, and hormonal cancers. My reasoning for the importance of this is because I am an auto worker thus we are told we are working with heavily cancerous materials. Furthermore, the study revealed elevated risks of stomach and kidney cancers... very scary. These illnesses were associated with higher levels of exposure to engine oil on the skin, and an increased incidence of rectal and prostate cancer with more exposure to ATF fluid. Additionally, synthetic oil was linked to a higher risk of non-Hodgkin lymphoma and prostate cancer. It's worth noting that no associations were found between engine oil and colon or lung related cancers.

This study struck fear into my brain. Many times I would get off work covered in oil, ATF fluid, brake cleaner, and other fluids, and yet I wouldn't always shower right away. I would grab some drinks with friends or even travel for extended periods of time. But now I shower practically immediately after getting off work. Thus, I found probability data of their findings to look at the higher probability of getting these cancers if exposed for long periods of time.
